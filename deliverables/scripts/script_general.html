Production Notes

Pacing

Total runtime: 155 seconds across 18 slides. Average ~8.6 seconds per slide. This version runs slightly warmer and more conversational than the technical script. Allow micro-pauses after culturally resonant lines — especially the “phone booth in 1993” opener and the “internet before the internet” closer.

Audience Calibration

Slides 6 (Architecture), 8 (How the Library Works), 10 (Engineering Quality), and 11 (System Design) are the four slides where technical content is translated for general audiences. The analogies — modular synthesizer, card catalog, wall outlet, tour bus — should be delivered with confidence, not apology. Do not preface with “to put it simply” or “in layman’s terms.” The analogies are the explanation.

Humor Placement

Four humor beats: “legibility was optional” (Slide 2), “drag and drop, the way nature intended” (Slide 3), “parking a tour bus in a compact spot” (Slide 14), and “shouldn’t require a subscription” (Slide 16). The Slide 14 line is the biggest laugh — give it a beat of silence after delivery.

Emotional Arc

Slides 1–3: enthusiasm and accessibility. Slides 4–6: credibility and scale. Slides 7–9: the “aha moment” — this is not just another AI tool. Slides 10–13: depth and ambition. Slides 14–16: resilience and transparency. Slides 17–18: community and legacy. The emotional peak is Slide 7 (digital librarian); the closing should feel like a quiet statement of purpose, not a crescendo.

Fallback if Running Long

Slides 10 (Engineering Quality) and 11 (System Design) can be compressed to 5–6 seconds each for mixed audiences. The analogies are strong enough to carry without elaboration. Slide 5 (Completeness) can also be trimmed — the curtains joke is charming but expendable.
