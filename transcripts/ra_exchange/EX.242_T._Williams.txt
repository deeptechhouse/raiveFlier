By the time he was out of his teens, Williams had already enjoyed huge success as part of Grimes' first wave, only to see the whole thing crumble around him as the music mutated and audiences moved on. When he re-emerged in 2010 under the name T. Williams after years spent honing his craft, his new house-led sound dovetailed neatly with the burgeoning UK funky movement. But by this point, Williams was operating free from any one sound or scene. He recorded a string of music for Tom Lee's local action label, including the smash vocal house track heartbeat. He eventually fell in with PMR Records, winding up touring the US with Labelmates Disclosure. These days, Williams is most interested in bringing the entire melting pot of UK club music together, which is why he started Today Tomorrow, a label that will embrace stylistic fusions and focus on new talent.
