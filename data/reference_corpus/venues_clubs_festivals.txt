Major Rave Venues, Clubs, and Festivals: A Global Reference

CLUBS AND VENUES

Berghain
Location: Am Wriezener Bahnhof, Friedrichshain, Berlin, Germany
Opened: 2004
Capacity: 1,500
Type: Techno/house club
Description: Housed in a former power station, Berghain is widely considered the world's greatest techno club. The main floor (Berghain) focuses on techno, while the upstairs Panorama Bar plays house music. Features a Funktion-One sound system. Known for its strict door policy enforced by bouncer Sven Marquardt, marathon opening hours (opens Friday midnight, closes Monday morning), and no-photography policy.
Residents: Marcel Dettmann, Ben Klock, Nd_Baumecker, Tama Sumo, Boris, Fiedel, Steffi
Label: Ostgut Ton
Notable events: Silvester (New Year), Klubnacht (regular weekends)

Tresor
Location: Kopenicker Strasse 70, Mitte, Berlin, Germany (original: Leipziger Strasse vault)
Opened: 1991 (original), 2007 (current location)
Capacity: 800 (original)
Type: Techno
Description: One of the first and most important techno clubs in Berlin. The original location was in the vault of the former Wertheim department store. Founded by Dimitri Hegemann. Forged a direct connection between Berlin and Detroit techno.
Residents/Regular guests: Jeff Mills, Juan Atkins, Blake Baxter, Surgeon, Regis
Label: Tresor Records

Fabric
Location: 77A Charterhouse Street, Clerkenwell, London, UK
Opened: 1999
Capacity: 2,500
Type: Electronic music (all genres)
Description: London's most iconic electronic music venue, housed in a former cold store. Famous for its Bodysonic dance floor (with bass transducers embedded in the floor) and Room One/Room Two/Room Three layout. Founded by Keith Reilly and Cameron Leslie.
Residents: Craig Richards, Terry Francis, Andy C (FABRICLIVE), Hessle Audio
Labels: fabric, FABRICLIVE (mix CD series)
Notable: Temporarily closed by Islington Council in 2016, saved by community campaign.

The Warehouse
Location: 206 South Jefferson Street, Chicago, Illinois, USA
Opened: 1977
Closed: 1983
Type: House music (origin)
Description: The nightclub where house music was born. DJ Frankie Knuckles held a residency playing disco, soul, and early electronic music to a predominantly Black and gay audience. The name "house music" derives from this venue.
Notable: After Knuckles left, it reopened as the Music Box under DJ Ron Hardy.

Paradise Garage
Location: 84 King Street, SoHo, Manhattan, New York, USA
Opened: 1977
Closed: 1987
Type: Disco/house/garage
Description: DJ Larry Levan's legendary residency defined the "garage" sound and set the standard for DJ-as-artist. Known for its Richard Long-designed sound system â€” one of the finest ever built. The club's predominantly Black and Latino, largely gay audience created a space of radical inclusivity.
Notable: Inspired Ministry of Sound's sound system design. "Garage" music takes its name from this club.

Space Ibiza
Location: Playa d'en Bossa, Ibiza, Spain
Opened: 1986
Closed: 2016
Type: House/techno
Description: Founded by Pepe Rosello. One of Ibiza's most iconic clubs, known for its open-air terrace and indoor main room. Carl Cox's "Music Is Revolution" residency (2001-2016) was the club's signature event.
Notable residents: Carl Cox, Fatboy Slim, Richie Hawtin
Notable: Named world's #1 club multiple times by DJ Magazine. Replaced by Hi Ibiza in 2017.

Amnesia
Location: San Rafael, Ibiza, Spain
Opened: 1976
Type: House/techno/trance
Description: One of Ibiza's original clubs, famous for its open-air terrace where DJ Alfredo played the eclectic "Balearic" sets that inspired the UK acid house movement. The main room (covered) hosts bigger acts while the terrace maintains a more intimate vibe.
Notable residents: DJ Alfredo, Marco Carola (Music On), Sven Vath (Cocoon)

DC10
Location: Near Ibiza airport, Ibiza, Spain
Opened: 1999
Type: Techno/house (underground)
Description: A converted airplane hangar near the airport, DC10 is Ibiza's most underground club. Its Monday Circoloco party, launched in 1999, became one of the island's most famous events.
Circoloco residents: Seth Troxler, Jamie Jones, The Martinez Brothers, tINI

Pacha
Location: Multiple locations (original: Sitges, 1967; Ibiza: 1973)
Type: House/disco/commercial
Description: The cherry logo is one of the most recognized brand symbols in nightlife. Ibiza Pacha has hosted major residencies including Solomun's +1 and Flower Power.

The Hacienda (FAC 51)
Location: 11-13 Whitworth Street West, Manchester, UK
Opened: 1982
Closed: 1997 (demolished 2002)
Type: Acid house/rave/indie dance
Description: Owned by Factory Records and New Order, The Hacienda became the epicenter of the "Madchester" scene. Mike Pickering's Nude night and Graeme Park's sets defined Manchester's acid house sound. Despite its iconic status, the club was never financially successful and was plagued by gang violence in its later years.

Shelter (formerly known as Sound Factory)
Location: 530 West 27th Street, Manhattan, New York, USA
Type: House
Description: Junior Vasquez's residency at Sound Factory in the 1990s set the standard for NYC house culture. The venue (under various names and operators) continued the NYC warehouse party tradition.

Womb
Location: Shibuya, Tokyo, Japan
Opened: 2000
Type: Techno/house
Description: Tokyo's premier electronic music club, featuring a massive main floor and a Funktion-One sound system. Regular host to international touring DJs.

FESTIVALS

Tomorrowland
Location: De Schorre recreation area, Boom, Belgium
Founded: 2005 (by ID&T Belgium)
Capacity: 400,000 (across two weekends)
Type: Multi-genre EDM
Description: The world's most famous electronic music festival, known for its elaborate mainstage designs with different themes each year (e.g., "The Book of Wisdom," "The Story of Planaxis"). Features 16+ stages across genres from techno to trance to hardstyle.

Awakenings
Location: Various (typically Spaarnwoude recreational area, Netherlands)
Founded: 1997
Type: Techno
Description: The Netherlands' premier techno festival. Known for booking the world's top techno DJs including Adam Beyer, Carl Cox, Ben Klock, and Nina Kraviz.

Sonar
Location: Barcelona, Spain
Founded: 1994
Type: Electronic music + digital arts
Description: Bridges electronic music with digital arts and technology. Split into Sonar by Day (exhibitions, talks) and Sonar by Night (music performances). One of the most culturally significant electronic music events.

Movement (DEMF)
Location: Hart Plaza, Detroit, Michigan, USA
Founded: 2000 (as DEMF)
Type: Techno/house/electronic
Description: Celebrates Detroit's techno heritage on the banks of the Detroit River. Features the Belleville Three, Underground Resistance, and Detroit legends alongside international acts.

Time Warp
Location: Maimarkthalle, Mannheim, Germany (also events in Netherlands, Brazil, USA)
Founded: 1994
Type: Techno/house
Description: Germany's longest-running indoor techno event. Known for exceptional production, sound quality, and consistently strong lineups.

Dekmantel
Location: Amsterdamse Bos, Amsterdam, Netherlands
Founded: 2013
Type: Techno/house/experimental
Description: Rapidly became one of Europe's most respected electronic music festivals, known for its adventurous programming bridging underground and experimental sounds.

Glastonbury (Silver Hayes)
Location: Worthy Farm, Pilton, Somerset, UK
Type: Multi-genre (electronic stages)
Description: While primarily a rock/pop festival, Glastonbury's Silver Hayes area (formerly Dance Village) has become one of the UK's most significant electronic music stages, hosting acts from Carl Cox to The Chemical Brothers.

Mysteryland
Location: Haarlemmermeer, Netherlands
Founded: 1993
Type: Multi-genre EDM
Description: The world's longest-running electronic music festival. Organized by ID&T.

Creamfields
Location: Daresbury, Cheshire, UK
Founded: 1998
Type: Multi-genre EDM
Description: The UK's largest dance music festival, spawned from the Cream superclub brand in Liverpool.

Burning Man
Location: Black Rock Desert, Nevada, USA
Founded: 1986
Type: Art/music/community (significant electronic music presence)
Description: While not strictly an electronic music festival, Burning Man's sound camps and art cars have made it a major destination for DJs. Robot Heart, Mayan Warrior, and Camp Question Mark are among the most famous sound camps.

SOUND SYSTEMS AND FREE PARTY CULTURE

Spiral Tribe
Origin: London, UK (later nomadic across Europe)
Active: 1990-present
Type: Free party sound system / tekno
Description: One of the most influential free party sound systems. After the Castlemorton Common Festival in 1992 (which partly inspired the Criminal Justice Act), several members relocated to France and continental Europe, spreading the free tekno movement.

DiY Sound System
Origin: Nottingham, UK
Active: 1989-present
Type: Free party / house / techno
Description: Nottingham's legendary sound system collective, organizing free outdoor parties that carried the original rave spirit.

Mutoid Waste Company
Origin: London, UK
Active: 1984-present
Type: Art/rave collective
Description: Combined scrap metal sculpture with rave culture. Their parties featured elaborate post-apocalyptic art installations alongside electronic music.

Desert Storm
Origin: Arizona/California, USA
Type: Desert rave sound system
Description: Part of the American desert rave tradition, where illegal parties were held in remote desert locations, influencing the aesthetic and ethos of events like Burning Man.
