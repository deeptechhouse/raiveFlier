Major DJs and Artists in Electronic Dance Music: A Reference Guide

This reference covers key DJs and producers across house, techno, trance, drum and bass, and related genres. Each entry includes origin, active years, key labels, signature style, and notable venues/residencies.

TECHNO

Jeff Mills
Origin: Detroit, Michigan, USA
Active: 1985-present
Labels: Axis Records (founder), Underground Resistance (co-founder), Purpose Maker
Style: Hard, driving techno with complex layering. Known for three-deck mixing with live TR-909. Minimal yet intensely physical.
Key releases: Waveform Transmission Vol. 1, The Exhibitionist, Metropolis
Residencies/Venues: Tresor (Berlin), Liquid Room (Tokyo), worldwide
Notable: Former radio DJ "The Wizard" on WJLB Detroit. Co-founded Underground Resistance with Mad Mike Banks.

Richie Hawtin
Origin: Banbury, England; raised in Windsor, Ontario, Canada (across river from Detroit)
Active: 1989-present
Labels: Plus 8 (co-founder with John Acquaviva), Minus (founder), M_nus
Style: Minimal techno pioneer. Known for innovative use of technology including Traktor, Ableton, and custom MIDI controllers. Releases as Plastikman feature deep, acid-influenced minimal techno.
Key releases: Sheet One (as Plastikman), Consumed, DE9 series
Residencies/Venues: Berghain, ENTER. at Space Ibiza, worldwide
Notable: Bridged Detroit and European techno scenes. Pioneer of minimal techno alongside Robert Hood.

Adam Beyer
Origin: Stockholm, Sweden
Active: 1993-present
Labels: Drumcode (founder), Truesoul
Style: Driving, percussion-heavy techno with industrial undertones. Drumcode became the world's best-selling techno label.
Key releases: Drumcode compilations, DC series
Residencies/Venues: Awakenings, Drumcode events worldwide
Notable: Built Drumcode into the largest techno brand globally.

Charlotte de Witte
Origin: Ghent, Belgium
Active: 2010-present
Labels: KNTXT (founder)
Style: Dark, industrial-influenced techno with acid elements.
Residencies/Venues: Tomorrowland, Awakenings, worldwide festivals
Notable: Named world's #1 female DJ multiple times. Known for headline festival sets.

Nina Kraviz
Origin: Irkutsk, Siberia, Russia
Active: 2008-present
Labels: Trip (founder)
Style: Raw, hypnotic techno with acid and psychedelic elements. Known for eclectic DJ sets spanning techno, acid, and electro.
Key releases: Nina Kraviz (debut album), "Ghetto Kraviz"
Residencies/Venues: Berghain, worldwide
Notable: Distinctive voice in both production and vocal tracks.

Amelie Lens
Origin: Antwerp, Belgium
Active: 2016-present
Labels: LENSKE (founder), Exhale
Style: Hard, pounding techno with rave influences.
Residencies/Venues: Awakenings, Tomorrowland, worldwide
Notable: Rapid rise to become one of techno's biggest draws.

HOUSE

Frankie Knuckles
Origin: The Bronx, New York; based in Chicago
Active: 1977-2014 (died 2014)
Labels: Trax Records, Def Mix Productions
Style: Soulful, gospel-influenced house. Pioneer of the genre. Known for warm, uplifting sets blending disco, soul, and electronic elements.
Key releases: "Your Love," "The Whistle Song," "Baby Wants to Ride"
Residencies/Venues: The Warehouse (Chicago), The Power Plant (Chicago), Sound Factory (NYC)
Notable: "Godfather of House Music." The Warehouse gave house music its name.

Larry Heard (Mr. Fingers)
Origin: Chicago, Illinois, USA
Active: 1984-present
Labels: Alleviated Records, Black Market International
Style: Deep house pioneer. Lush, melodic, and soulful. Created the blueprint for deep house with warm pads and gentle rhythms.
Key releases: "Can You Feel It," "Mystery of Love," Amnesia, Introduction
Residencies/Venues: Chicago clubs, worldwide
Notable: "Can You Feel It" is considered one of the greatest house tracks ever made.

Kerri Chandler
Origin: East Orange, New Jersey, USA
Active: 1991-present
Labels: Madhouse Records (founder), Kaoz Theory
Style: Deep, soulful house with jazz and gospel influences. Known for Moog synthesizer mastery and powerful bass-heavy productions.
Key releases: Atmosphere series, "Rain," "Bar a Thym"
Residencies/Venues: Worldwide, particularly NYC and European clubs
Notable: One of the most respected figures in deep house.

Seth Troxler
Origin: Kalamazoo, Michigan, USA
Active: 2006-present
Labels: Visionquest (co-founder)
Style: Deep, eclectic house and techno with storytelling sensibility. Known for long, narrative DJ sets.
Residencies/Venues: DC10 (Ibiza), worldwide
Notable: Named world's #1 DJ by multiple publications.

The Black Madonna (The Blessed Madonna)
Origin: Lexington, Kentucky, USA; based in Chicago
Active: 2010-present
Labels: We Still Believe
Style: High-energy house with disco, gospel, and rave influences. Known for joyful, transcendent sets.
Residencies/Venues: Smart Bar (Chicago), worldwide
Notable: Championed inclusivity and diversity in electronic music.

TRANCE

Paul van Dyk
Origin: East Berlin, Germany
Active: 1991-present
Labels: Vandit Records (founder)
Style: Melodic, uplifting trance with emotional depth. Pioneer of the Berlin trance sound.
Key releases: 45 RPM, Out There and Back, "For an Angel"
Residencies/Venues: Tresor (Berlin), Love Parade, worldwide
Notable: First artist to receive a Grammy nomination for Best Dance/Electronic Album.

Armin van Buuren
Origin: Leiden, Netherlands
Active: 1995-present
Labels: Armada Music (co-founder)
Style: Uplifting and progressive trance. Built A State of Trance (ASOT) radio show into a global brand with 40+ million listeners.
Key releases: 76, Imagine, "Communication," ASOT compilations
Residencies/Venues: Amnesia (Ibiza), ASOT events worldwide
Notable: Named world's #1 DJ five times. ASOT is the world's most popular dance music radio show.

Above & Beyond (Jono Grant, Tony McGuinness, Paavo Siljamaki)
Origin: London, UK
Active: 2000-present
Labels: Anjunabeats, Anjunadeep
Style: Progressive and uplifting trance with emotional, vocal-driven tracks.
Key releases: Tri-State, Group Therapy, "Sun & Moon"
Residencies/Venues: Group Therapy events worldwide, ABGT festivals
Notable: Built Anjunabeats/Anjunadeep into one of the most beloved label ecosystems in dance music.

DRUM AND BASS / JUNGLE

LTJ Bukem
Origin: London, UK
Active: 1990-present
Labels: Good Looking Records, Looking Good Records
Style: Atmospheric, jazzy drum and bass. Pioneer of "intelligent" or "liquid" drum and bass.
Key releases: "Demon's Theme," "Atlantis," Logical Progression compilations
Residencies/Venues: Speed at the Mars Bar (London), worldwide
Notable: Good Looking Records defined the liquid/atmospheric DnB sound.

Andy C
Origin: London, UK
Active: 1991-present
Labels: Ram Records (founder)
Style: High-energy, technical drum and bass DJ known for rapid-fire mixing and beat-juggling.
Residencies/Venues: Fabric (London), XOYO residency, worldwide
Notable: Possibly the most decorated DJ in drum and bass history. Ram Records released Chase & Status, Sub Focus, and Wilkinson.

Roni Size
Origin: Bristol, UK
Active: 1992-present
Labels: Full Cycle Records (co-founder), V Recordings
Style: Breakbeat-heavy, live-instrument-infused drum and bass. His group Reprazent blended DnB with live bass, drums, and vocals.
Key releases: New Forms (Mercury Prize winner, 1997)
Residencies/Venues: Bristol and worldwide
Notable: New Forms won the Mercury Music Prize, the first DnB album to achieve major industry recognition.

DJ Marky
Origin: Sao Paulo, Brazil
Active: 1995-present
Labels: Innerground Records
Style: Liquid, Brazilian-influenced drum and bass. Known for melodic, soulful sets with Brazilian rhythms.
Key releases: "LK (Carolina Carol Bela)" — massive international DnB hit
Residencies/Venues: Fabric (London), worldwide
Notable: Helped establish Brazil as a major drum and bass market.

CARL COX — SPECIAL EXTENDED ENTRY

Carl Cox
Origin: Oldham, Greater Manchester, England; raised in Brighton, East Sussex
Born: July 29, 1962
Active: 1985-present
Labels: Intec Records (founder), 23rd Century Records (founder)
Style: Broad-spectrum electronic music encompassing techno, house, tribal, and acid. Known for marathon 8+ hour sets, three-deck mixing technique, and infectious energy. Cox's style bridges the groove of house with the intensity of techno.

Early career: Cox began DJing at age 15, playing soul and funk at house parties in Brighton. He was among the first DJs to play acid house in the UK, performing at early events including Shoom and the Sunrise raves. He was one of the first DJs to use three turntables simultaneously.

Key releases: "I Want You (Forever)" (1991, reached UK #23), "Does It Feel Good to You" (1992), At the End of the Cul-de-Sac (album), The Second Chapter (album), "Phoebe" (as 23rd Century).

Residencies: Space Ibiza — Cox's "Music Is Revolution" residency at Space Ibiza from 2001-2016 is one of the most legendary residencies in clubbing history. The Tuesday night events became a pilgrimage for techno and house fans from around the world. After Space closed, he moved to Privilege and later Hi Ibiza. Also held residencies at The Arches (Melbourne), and promoted Global events worldwide.

Production/Labels: Founded Intec Records in 1999, which became a leading techno imprint releasing music by Jon Rundell, Marco Bailey, Harvey McKay, Nicole Moudaber, and others. Intec Digital expanded the label's reach.

Radio: Hosted "Carl Cox Global" radio show (later "Global with Carl Cox"), broadcast in over 30 countries, showcasing new and underground techno and house music.

Festival appearances: Regular headliner at Ultra Music Festival (Miami), Tomorrowland, Awakenings, Glastonbury (Silver Hayes), Burning Man, Coachella, and virtually every major electronic music festival worldwide.

Awards/Recognition: Named world's #1 DJ multiple times. Received the IDMA (International Dance Music Awards) Lifetime Achievement Award. Known by the nickname "The King of Techno" and "The Three Deck Wizard."

Cultural significance: Cox is widely regarded as one of the most important DJs in the history of electronic music. His career spans the full evolution of rave culture from illegal acid house parties to global festivals. His inclusive, joyful approach to DJing — often grinning behind the decks and encouraging the crowd — has made him beloved across generations of ravers. His catchphrase "Oh yes, oh yes!" has become iconic.
