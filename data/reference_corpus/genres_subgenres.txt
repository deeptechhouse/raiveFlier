Electronic Dance Music Genres and Subgenres: A Reference Guide

HOUSE MUSIC (118-135 BPM typically)

House
Origin: Chicago, 1984-1986
Characteristics: 4/4 kick drum pattern, synthesized basslines, hi-hat patterns, often with soulful or gospel-influenced vocals. Warm, danceable, and groove-oriented.
Key artists: Frankie Knuckles, Marshall Jefferson, Larry Heard, Ron Hardy

Deep House (118-125 BPM)
Origin: Chicago/New York, mid-1980s
Characteristics: Deeper basslines, more atmospheric pads, jazz and soul influences. Less percussive than standard house, more emphasis on mood and texture.
Key artists: Larry Heard, Kerri Chandler, Ron Trent, Moodymann, Theo Parrish

Acid House (120-135 BPM)
Origin: Chicago, 1987
Characteristics: Defined by the squelchy, resonant sound of the Roland TB-303 bass synthesizer. Repetitive, hypnotic patterns with filter sweeps.
Key artists: Phuture (DJ Pierre), Adonis, Bam Bam, Armando
Key tracks: "Acid Tracks" (Phuture), "No Way Back" (Adonis)

Progressive House (120-132 BPM)
Origin: UK, early 1990s
Characteristics: Long, evolving track structures with gradual builds and breakdowns. More melodic and atmospheric than standard house. Often features extended intros and outros for DJ mixing.
Key artists: Sasha, John Digweed, Hernan Cattaneo, Nick Warren

Tech House (124-130 BPM)
Origin: UK/Europe, mid-1990s
Characteristics: Fusion of house's groove with techno's minimalism and darker tones. Repetitive, functional, DJ-oriented.
Key artists: Mr. G, Jamie Jones, Patrick Topping, Hot Since 82

Garage / UK Garage (130-140 BPM)
Origin: New York (garage) / London (UK garage), 1990s
Characteristics: NYC garage derived from Larry Levan's Paradise Garage sets — soulful, vocal-heavy. UK garage (also called 2-step) features shuffled, syncopated rhythms, R&B vocals, and skippy beats.
Key artists: Todd Edwards, MJ Cole, Artful Dodger, El-B, Wookie, Tuff Jam

Afro House (118-128 BPM)
Origin: South Africa/Africa, 2000s
Characteristics: House music infused with African rhythms, percussion, and melodies. Organic textures, tribal drums, call-and-response vocals.
Key artists: Black Coffee, Culoe De Song, Da Capo, Shimza

TECHNO (125-150 BPM typically)

Techno
Origin: Detroit, 1985-1988
Characteristics: Repetitive 4/4 beats, synthesized melodies and textures, futuristic and mechanical feel. Emphasis on rhythm and atmosphere over melody.
Key artists: Juan Atkins, Derrick May, Kevin Saunderson, Jeff Mills

Minimal Techno (125-135 BPM)
Origin: Detroit/Berlin, early-mid 1990s
Characteristics: Stripped-down techno with emphasis on subtle variations in rhythm, tone, and texture. Less is more — tracks evolve through micro-changes rather than dramatic builds.
Key artists: Robert Hood, Richie Hawtin, Ricardo Villalobos, Zip
Key releases: Minimal Nation (Robert Hood)

Industrial Techno (130-145 BPM)
Origin: UK/Berlin, 1990s-2000s
Characteristics: Harsh, abrasive textures borrowed from industrial music. Distorted kicks, metallic percussion, dark atmospheres.
Key artists: Surgeon, Regis, Female, Ancient Methods, Ansom

Dub Techno (120-132 BPM)
Origin: Berlin, early 1990s
Characteristics: Fusion of techno with dub reggae production techniques — heavy reverb, delay, echo, and sub-bass. Spacious, meditative.
Key artists: Basic Channel (Moritz von Oswald & Mark Ernestus), Chain Reaction, Rhythm & Sound, DeepChord

Hard Techno (140-160 BPM)
Origin: Europe, 1990s
Characteristics: Faster, more aggressive techno with pounding kicks and distortion. Often played at peak time in clubs.
Key artists: Amelie Lens, FJAAK, 999999999, Sara Landry

Acid Techno (130-150 BPM)
Origin: UK/Europe, early 1990s
Characteristics: Techno built around the Roland TB-303 acid sound. More driving and darker than acid house.
Key artists: Chris Liberator, D.A.V.E. The Drummer, Hardfloor, Luke Slater

TRANCE (128-150 BPM typically)

Trance
Origin: Germany (Frankfurt/Berlin), early 1990s
Characteristics: Melodic, euphoric, and uplifting. Long build-ups leading to emotional breakdowns and climaxes. Heavy use of synthesizer pads, arpeggios, and reverb.
Key artists: Paul van Dyk, Sven Vath, Paul Oakenfold, Tiesto, Armin van Buuren

Goa Trance / Psytrance (135-150 BPM)
Origin: Goa, India / Israel, late 1980s-1990s
Characteristics: Psychedelic, hypnotic, with rapid arpeggiated synthesizer lines, Eastern-influenced melodies, and trippy atmospheres. Goa trance is the earlier, more melodic form; psytrance is the harder evolution.
Key artists: Hallucinogen (Simon Posford), Infected Mushroom, Astral Projection, Shpongle

Progressive Trance (128-138 BPM)
Origin: Europe, mid-1990s
Characteristics: More subtle and evolving than uplifting trance. Longer tracks with gradual progression, less emphasis on breakdowns.
Key artists: Sasha, John Digweed, Hernan Cattaneo (crossover with progressive house)

DRUM AND BASS / JUNGLE (160-180 BPM)

Jungle
Origin: London, 1991-1995
Characteristics: Breakbeat-driven (often using the "Amen break" from The Winstons), heavy sub-bass, reggae/dancehall influences, chopped and time-stretched vocal samples. Fast tempos with half-time bass.
Key artists: Rebel MC, General Levy, Congo Natty (Rebel MC), A Guy Called Gerald, 4hero

Drum and Bass (170-180 BPM)
Origin: London/Bristol, 1995-present
Characteristics: Evolution of jungle with more refined production. Fast breakbeats, deep sub-bass, complex sound design. Multiple subgenres from liquid to neurofunk.
Key artists: Goldie, Roni Size, LTJ Bukem, Andy C, DJ Marky, Calibre

Liquid Drum and Bass (170-174 BPM)
Origin: London, mid-1990s
Characteristics: Melodic, soulful, and musical DnB. Jazz and soul influences, smooth basslines, emotive vocals.
Key artists: LTJ Bukem, Calibre, High Contrast, London Elektricity, Etherwood

Neurofunk (174-180 BPM)
Origin: UK, late 1990s
Characteristics: Dark, complex, and technically demanding DnB. Heavy bass design, robotic textures, intricate drum programming.
Key artists: Ed Rush & Optical, Noisia, Black Sun Empire, Phace

Jump-up (170-176 BPM)
Origin: UK, mid-1990s
Characteristics: Simpler, more dancefloor-friendly DnB with bouncy basslines and DJ-oriented structures.
Key artists: DJ Hype, DJ Zinc, Shy FX

HARDCORE AND VARIANTS

Hardcore (155-180 BPM)
Origin: UK/Netherlands, 1991-1993
Characteristics: Fast, energetic, piano stabs, breakbeats, vocal samples. "Happy hardcore" features uplifting melodies and pitch-shifted vocals.
Key artists: Hixxy, Dougal, Vibes, DJ Sy

Gabber (160-200+ BPM)
Origin: Rotterdam, Netherlands, 1992
Characteristics: Extremely fast, aggressive, distorted kick drums. Minimal melody, maximum intensity.
Key artists: Paul Elstak, Rotterdam Terror Corps, Neophyte, Angerfist
Events: Thunderdome, Masters of Hardcore

Breakcore (160-300+ BPM)
Origin: UK/Europe, mid-1990s
Characteristics: Chaotic, sample-heavy, combining extreme speed with humor and unpredictability. Often uses chopped-up Amen breaks at extreme tempos.
Key artists: Venetian Snares, Squarepusher, Shitmat, Bong-Ra

DUBSTEP AND BASS MUSIC

Dubstep (138-142 BPM)
Origin: South London, early 2000s
Characteristics: Heavy sub-bass, sparse rhythms, dark atmospheres, half-time beats. Original dubstep was minimal and dub-influenced.
Key artists: Skream, Benga, Digital Mystikz (Mala & Coki), Burial, Kode9
Key venue: FWD>> at Plastic People, London

Brostep / American Dubstep (140-150 BPM)
Origin: USA, 2010-2013
Characteristics: More aggressive, mid-range focused version of dubstep with "wobble bass" and filthy drops. Polarizing within the electronic music community.
Key artists: Skrillex, Excision, Bassnectar

UK Bass (130-140 BPM)
Origin: London, late 2000s
Characteristics: Umbrella term for bass-heavy UK electronic music that doesn't fit neatly into other categories. Often combines elements of garage, dubstep, house, and grime.
Key artists: Joy Orbison, Pearson Sound, Objekt, Pangaea

OTHER SIGNIFICANT GENRES

Breakbeat (120-140 BPM)
Origin: UK, late 1980s-1990s
Characteristics: Uses broken (non-4/4) beat patterns. Energetic and funky.
Key artists: The Chemical Brothers, Fatboy Slim, The Prodigy, Adam Freeland

Electro (110-150 BPM)
Origin: New York/Detroit, early 1980s
Characteristics: Based on Roland TR-808 drum machine patterns and vocoder vocals. Robotic, funky, and rhythmically complex.
Key artists: Afrika Bambaataa, Cybotron, Egyptian Lover, Drexciya

IDM (Intelligent Dance Music) (variable BPM)
Origin: UK, early 1990s
Characteristics: Experimental, complex, and often not designed for dancing. Intricate programming, unusual time signatures, abstract sound design.
Key artists: Aphex Twin, Autechre, Boards of Canada, Squarepusher
Key label: Warp Records

Ambient (variable BPM / beatless)
Origin: UK, 1970s-present (electronic ambient from early 1990s)
Characteristics: Atmospheric, beatless or with very subtle rhythms. Designed for immersive listening rather than dancing. Often features long, evolving textures and natural sounds.
Key artists: Brian Eno, The Orb, Aphex Twin (Selected Ambient Works), Biosphere, Stars of the Lid
